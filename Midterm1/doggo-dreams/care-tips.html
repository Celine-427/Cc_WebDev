Pet Care Tips

When a dog first arrives at home, it is recommended to keep them in a crate to make future training easier.

It is normal for a dog to bark randomly. If that happens, you can place a cloth over the crate.

If the puppy has not been dewormed yet, it is best to avoid taking them outside to prevent bacterial infections.

Puppies are prone to catching a chill. It is advised to wait for about a week and ensure they are healthy before bathing them.

Dogs need monthly internal and external deworming treatments.

Before the puppy arrives, make sure to have puppy food ready (a dog daily food intake is typically 2.5% to 4% of its body weight).

The golden period for dog training is within their first year.

It is important to prepare puppy pads, bowls, a dog bed, and other essentials in advance.
